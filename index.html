The Food Security Challenge

Under Malaysia's National Agrofood Policy 2021-2030 (DAN 2.0), increasing our rice self-sufficiency level (SSL) is crucial. High-yield cultivars like MR219 require precise management.

"Evaluating growth using aboveground biomass dry weight ($g/m^2$) allows smart agricultural systems to apply inputs right when the Crop Growth Rate (CGR) is at its absolute peak."

Mathematical Model

1. Cumulative Biomass $f(t)$

A Logistic Growth (Sigmoid) Curve modeling total dry matter accumulate up to 120 Days After Transplanting (DAT):

$$f(t) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}$$
$L = 1200$Max Biomass ($g/m^2$)
$k = 0.08$Growth Rate coeff.
$t_0 = 60$Inflection (DAT)

2. First Derivative $f'(t)$

The instantaneous growth rate (Crop Growth Rate, CGR, in $g/m^2/day$), obtained using the chain and quotient rules:

$$f'(t) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t - t_0)}}{\left(1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}\right)^2}$$

Calculus concept: While $f(t)$ calculates "how much" biomass is present, $f'(t)$ measures "how fast" the paddy is accumulating mass at exactly $t$ days.

What this Derivative means (Layman terms):

Think of cumulative biomass like a car's odometer (distance traveled), while the derivative is the speedometer (how fast the car is moving right now). Right now, the paddy is accelerating its growth rapidly.

Field Action Guide (SFM Precision Farming):

This is the critical stage of vegetative acceleration. Nitrogen-based top-dressing should be applied during high-rate periods to maximize grain spikelet formation before growth rate drops.
